### The matcher line (D-02 — PRESERVE exactly)

The shape-fence relaxation is a parser-pipeline filter step. It removes
statements from the `stmts` array; it does NOT evaluate any of them,
NOT side-channel them, NOT recurse into them. The user's intent for
`all(x=>x.punchcard())` is that the Strudel runtime EXECUTES it at
eval time (which it still does — the original `code` string is
unchanged); the parser's job is to MODEL the musical IR, which a
discardable side-effect statement has zero impact on. The matcher
line holds: structure modelled, side-effect never evaluated by the
parser.

### Composition with 20-18 (Chesterton — COMPOSE, do NOT SUBSUME)

The 20-18 `CHAIN_ROOT_RECOGNISER` curated Map is for STRUCTURED-PATTERN
roots (chord, arrange, irand, sine, …). The 20-19 curated regex is
for SIDE-EFFECT-ONLY statement heads (all, samples, setcps, …). These
are DIFFERENT classes (per the D-01 RATIONALE explicit rejection of
"compose with 20-18 CHAIN_ROOT_RECOGNISER"). They operate at different
pipeline stages (chain-root recognition is inside `parseRoot` /
`parseExpression`; the new filter is inside `buildBindingMap`'s
preamble). No 20-18 curated-Map line is touched.
